Rooted in Cincinnati and driven by our global experience, von Euw Tetrault is an architecture and design practice focused on contemporary solutions for buildings, landscapes, and the public realm. Our multidisciplinary backgrounds underpin and inform our approach to design, resulting in innovative and customized solutions for each project. We are unbounded by project type, category, or scale. Every challenge receives a unique perspective, without preconceptions. Our best work is done with curious clients who expect a completely fresh approach to each project. Our greatest satisfaction comes from moments of shared discovery leading to innovation and project breakthroughs. Our emphasis is on contextually driven design solutions. Projects are meticulously tailored and advanced by a deep understanding of the environment they inhabit. With nearly three decades of combined professional experience and a portfolio spanning 12 countries across five continents, we take pride in our track record of delivering exceptional buildings and landscapes. von Euw Tetrault is MBE, WBE, SBE, and EDGE certified.

VON EUW

Federica von Euw graduated from Swarthmore College with a Bachelor of Arts Degree in 2007 and the University of Pennsylvania in 2011 with a Masters of Architecture degree. Immediately thereafter she began collaborating at Herzog & de Meuron in Basel, Switzerland, where she contributed to, and led, a wide range of projects located in Switzerland, Italy, and the United States. Her notable projects include the Asklepios 8 office building on the Novartis Campus in Basel, Switzerland, a multi-use building for Feltrinelli Books in Milan, Italy, and the 1111 Lincoln Road Extension in Miami. Due to her international background, Federica speaks English, Spanish, German, Swiss German, and French fluently. Federica is a licensed architect in the state of Ohio.

TETRAULT

Andrew Tetrault received his Masters of Architecture from the University of Pennsylvania in 2011 and a Bachelors Degree in Landscape Architecture from Purdue University in 2008. He has collaborated with the Olin Studio in Philadelphia, Studio Andrew Todd in Paris, and Albaker Architects in Doha. In 2013 Andrew joined the office of Herzog & de Meuron in Basel, Switzerland, as an architect, collaborating on a range of projects from a museum and park in Doha, to the redevelopment of the Stamford Bridge Stadium for Chelsea F.C. in London.
